About


Re-CoLi is a project which aims to push the boundaries of lithium battery recycling.


Your phone, your watch, and maybe even your car will contain rechargeable lithium batteries. The device on which you are reading this is likely to be powered by one. The number of devices using lithium ion batteries has rocketed exponentially in recent years. This has not only created a strain on the environment but has created a burden for future generations to deal with.


Battery waste. There are currently no environmentally responsible methods that deal with lithium batteries after they have run their course. Not only do dead batteries scar our landscape in the dump, they contain toxic chemicals that can drain into the soil and contaminate water sources. We also shouldn’t forget about the high risk of fire and explosion they pose.


More than 10 million tonnes of used batteries are expected to be thrown out in the next 5 years, yet we have no systems in place to deal with them.


If something is to be done, action must be taken now. This is where we come in.


Our Goals


Currently it is still cheaper to mine lithium and cobalt than to recycle it. Our primary goals address the current state of research into recycling technology with the long-term vision to see all lithium batteries recycled:


  • • Develop an environmentally responsible process for reclaiming mineral resources found in Li-ion batteries
  • • Optimise our process in order to keep economic cost to a minimum and maximise profitability
  • • Ensure that our process is scalable, to be implemented on an industrial scale
